The Last of Us Part II

A gripping tale overshadowed by forced agendas.

3/5 Scoops
90% Woke

Introduction

The Last of Us Part II is the highly anticipated sequel to one of the most beloved games of the previous generation. Developed by Naughty Dog, it promises intense storytelling, brutal combat, and stunning visuals. However, it also dives headfirst into controversial themes that many feel push a "woke" agenda, detracting from what could have been a masterpiece.

Story and Characters

The narrative follows Ellie and Abby in a post-apocalyptic world, exploring themes of revenge, loss, and redemption. While the writing is top-notch and the performances are Oscar-worthy, the story often feels manipulated to promote diversity and inclusion at the expense of organic character development. Forced LGBTQ+ representation and gender role reversals come across as preachy rather than authentic, turning a potentially epic tale into a lecture on social issues.

Gameplay

Combat is visceral and satisfying, with improved stealth mechanics and a variety of weapons. Exploration in the ruined world is rewarding, filled with hidden secrets and emotional moments. However, some sections drag on, and the game's length feels padded to emphasize its messaging.

Graphics and Sound

Visually stunning, with lifelike animations and detailed environments. The sound design immerses you in the horror, from the clickers' eerie sounds to the emotional score. Technically, it's a triumph.
